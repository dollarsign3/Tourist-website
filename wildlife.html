Wildlife attraction Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is not a god to any country in terms of biodiversity. Also, the best country for safaris out of the African continent is Sri Lanka.There are many National parks in Sri Lanka such as Sinharaja, Wilpattu, Yala, Kumana etc.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is located in Sri Lanka's southeast and spans two provinces: the Hambantota district in the southern province as well as Monaragala district in the Uva province. Colombo is 305 kilometers from the entrance point. Yala National Park is the greatest place to see Sri Lanka's diverse range of animals. A flock of brightly painted storks can be seen sitting on the lagoon's banks, where the crocodiles have decided to sleep. In the woods, where monkeys swing, bounce, and talk, lovely fan-tailed peacocks in vibrant blues and greens parade around.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Sinharaja Forest Reserve, which is located in Sabaragamuwa and the Southern provinces in Sri Lanka's southwest lowland wet zone, is a major biodiversity hotspot. The Sinharaja Forest Reserve has a total area of 7,648 hectares. The annual rainfall in Sinharaja Woods ranges from 3614mm to 5006mm, with temperatures ranging from 19°C to 34°C. The maximum rainfall occurs during the southwest monsoon season in May-July and the northeastern monsoon season in November-January. The Ceylon Hanging Parrot (Loriculus beryllium), Ashy-headed Laughing Thrush (Garrulax cinereifrons), Layard's parakeet (Psittacula calthripae), Ceylon Wood Pigeon (Columba Torrington), and other endemic species may be seen in Sinharaja Rain Forest.

Wilpattu National Park

Wilpattu National Park is regarded as one of Sri Lanka's most beautiful national parks. The presence of natural lakes, sand-rimmed water basins, or depressions that fill with rain is a distinctive characteristic of this park. Wilpattu is one of Sri Lanka's oldest and largest national parks. Elephants, sloth bears, water buffalo, and spotted deer are just a few of the creatures found in Sri Lanka. It is located in Sri Lanka's North Western Province, in an arid zone. The park spans 131,696 hectares.
