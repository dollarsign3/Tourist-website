Activities in Sri Lanka

For foreigners, Sri Lanka has lots of fun and adventurous activities. Especially places like Kithulgala, Sigiriya, Arugam bay, and Yala National Park have rafting, hot air balloons, surfing, and safaris.

White Water Rafting

  • Location: Kithulgala

  • Season: If you're seeking for the finest time to go Kitulgala rafting, the months of December to May are ideal because the water levels are high and the rapids may be enjoyed.

  • Activities and Cost: White water rafting (From US$30 pp), Cycling (From US$40 pp), confidence jump (From US$30 pp), Bird Watching (From US$35 pp)
    Visit for the further details

White Water Rafting is a thrilling water sport and a challenging leisure activity. However, contrary to popular assumptions, White Water Rafting is not a physically demanding activity. A good level of physical condition and a positive attitude would suffice. White Water Rafting, which can be done in both whitewater and other turbulent waters, provides an exciting experience for water sports lovers. In Sri Lanka, the primary attraction in Kithulgala is white water rafting, where you may ride the Kelani River's tides. Aside from that, it's known for activities like confidence jumping, stream sliding, waterfall abseiling, jungle trekking, Bird viewing, cave exploring, river outings, and adventures are all available.

Hot Air Balloon Rides

  • Location: Dambulla

  • Available: December to March

  • Flight Duration: 1 hour plus or minus 10 minutes

  • Pricing: US$210 per adult person and US$160 per child

  • Additional: Hotel transfers, Breakfast, After flight celebration, Insurance included for packages.
    Visit for the further details

Hot air ballooning, drifting gently over the cultural triangle or a wildlife park in a hot air balloon, is a great way to experience the exhilaration of flight with a bird's eye view of the area below, a silent ride through the sky, and an excursion that has rapidly become popular with locals and tourists alike. Under the blue sky and over a landscape dotted with several little man-made lakes, hot air balloons carried away; one might even sight an elephant near these tanks, old monuments. The service is now available from December to March and also is located in Dambulla.

Safari

  • Locations: Yala National park, Minneriya National Park, Udawalawe National Park,

  • Ride Duration: 4 - 12 hours

  • Pricing: Rs.9000 - Rs.18,000

    Visit for the further details

When we think of safaris, we usually think about Africa, but don't underestimate the splendor of a Sri Lanka safari. Due to its wealth of national parks, rich wildlife – including elephants and leopards – and sustainable conservation approach, Sri Lanka has become one of the most sought-after safari destinations in the world. More than 400 bird species, as well as elephants, leopards, sloth bears, blue whales, and sperm whales, can be found there. It's also one of the rare spots where you can go whale watching and go on a land safari all in one day.

Hiking

  • Locations: Knuckles Range, Pidurangala, Adam’s Peak, Sigiriya

  • Duration: It changes depending on places.

  • Pricing: US$30 onwards

  • Additional: Coffee/Tea, Snacks, Bottled Water included for the packages.
    Visit for the further details

The diversified and beautiful hilly terrain of Sri Lanka offers a wealth of hiking opportunities for the avid hiker. The gorgeous areas of Haputale and Diyatalawa, in Sri Lanka's hill country, provide a good starting point for a range of hiking and trekking treks through the jungle, tea estates, and up mountains. Another popular attraction in Sri Lanka is the river as well as several waterfalls, which allow hikers and trekkers to relax while on the trail! It's better to start ascending the renowned Adam's Peak Wilderness Sanctuary at night so you can arrive at the top in time for a stunning sunrise when the peak's mystical shadow is perfectly projected across the clouds. A hiking tour in Sri Lanka isn't complete without a stop at the fascinating Sinharaja Rain Forest, where a selection of trails can be tailored to the group's specific interests and fitness levels.
